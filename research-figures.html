The full prescribed sequence (LOOIE → SAURY → CHANT → BIDED → PRIMP) applied to OMEGA. Every guess yields only yellow and gray tiles — no green letter ever registers, requiring participants to override the goal-gradient instinct for all five turns before solving. OMEGA was one of six target words randomly drawn from the 300 New York Times daily Wordles released before the cutoff date; its alignment with the prescribed sequence was incidental, not selected.

All nine guided participants who played OMEGA reached the six-turn ceiling — none solved within the allotted turns. The guided mean of 6.00 against a non-guided mean of 5.17 for the same word represents the starkest word-level gap in the dataset (see Table 2). Because OMEGA occupied a guided session slot for 9 of 20 participants, this single word contributes disproportionately to the guided condition’s elevated overall mean of 5.40. The non-guided participants who played OMEGA retained the flexibility to adjust their guesses after early feedback; guided participants had five of their six turns committed to the prescribed sequence regardless of feedback direction.
